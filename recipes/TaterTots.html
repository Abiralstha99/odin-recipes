Tater Tots

Description

Test Kitchen Tips “You can make a lot of tater tots for not a lot of money,” according to culinary producer and test kitchen expert Nicole McLaughlin (a.k.a. NicoleMcMom). Here are some of her favorite tips and tricks for perfect tots every time:

  1. Don't overcook the potatoes to keep them shreddable.
  2. Use the microwave to save time.
  3. Use a slotted spoon to prevent oil splashes.

What to serve with Tater Tots

Pair the tater tots with ketchup or your favorite homemade dipping sauce — check out our Best Copycat Sauces From Your Favorite Restaurants for delicious inspiration.

How to Store Tater Tots

Store your leftover tater tots in an airtight container lined with paper towels for up to a week. Reheat in the microwave, oven, or air fryer.

Ingredients

  • 2 medium russet potatoes, peeled
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley
  • 1 tablespoon all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt or to taste
  • 1 teaspoon seasoned pepper
  • ½ teaspoon onion powder
  • 2 cups peanut oil

Steps

  1. Place potatoes in a large pot and cover with water; bring to a boil. Reduce heat to medium-low and simmer until tender, about 10 minutes; drain and cool until easily handled, 10 to 15 minutes.
  2. Shred potatoes with a box grater; drain excess water. Transfer shredded potato into a large mixing bowl; stir in parsley, flour, salt, seasoned pepper, and onion powder until combined. Shape potato mixture into 1-inch long potato nuggets.
  3. Heat oil in a deep fryer or large saucepan to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).
  4. Lower potato nuggets carefully into the hot oil in batches, about 5 to 10 at a time. Fry until golden brown and crispy, about 3 to 4 minutes. Transfer to a paper towel-lined plate to drain. Repeat with remaining nuggets.
